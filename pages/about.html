Bio

Hi! I'm Alexander Winslow Robinson Bean (he/him). There is plenty to know about me, which I hope I'll be able to go over here in no particular order.

Gaming

I am a long time gamer, I played my first video game -- Final Fantasy IX for the PS1 -- when I was five. Saying I "played" it, though, might be a bit of an exaggeration; I'd mostly make my dad run around in the overworld until he got into a random encounter and then I would take over. I enjoyed the fighting, while the actual random encounter screen swirl of the time would always startle me. My love for Final Fantasy has continued, however, as I regularly play FFXIV and I try to play every new mainline Final Fantasy release.
Aside from Final Fantasy, I have a decently wide range of games that I enjoy. Here's a small sampler of games that I've played recently that I'd recommend:

  • Final Fantasy XIV (obviously)
  • Street Fighter 6
  • Soulash 2
  • Songs of Syx
  • Albion Online
  • Century: Age of Ashes

There are plenty of others that I've played that I would certainly recommend, but the list would end up taking the entire page. Maybe I'll make a separate page dedicated to video game reviews.

Programming & Computers

This one is probably pretty apparent within the small web community that any given person likely has some interest in programming or computers, so I'll try to keep it brief.
I started out my interest in computers hand-in-hand with video gaming earlier in my life. I don't remember how I originally got them, but I would play my copies of Age of Empires, Rome: Total War, and Neverwinter Nights on the family Dell when I lived in Las Vegas.
This would snowball into further interests in PC gaming and thusly the computer itself. Living now in California, my dad would offer for my birthday to help me choose parts for and build my first custom computer. The most I remember about my original build is that it had an AMD CPU and GPU since they were cheaper at the time, and I barely helped build it because it confused me. I took that build with me through our future moves and, if I remember correctly, is a bit of a Ship of Theseus to my current PC build now.
Interest in computers blossomed into an interest in programming, encouraged by my dad once again. I took every programming class in high school that I could, learning Java, C++, HTML, CSS, a bit of game development with GameMaker, probably a couple others as well. That interest carried through into college at the Oregon Institute of Technology where I started out as a CS major, but then moved to Embedded System Engineering Technology after my first year, preferring the idea of working with microcontrollers and other small electronics.
Today, I work as a Controls Engineer in automation. My job is done one part in front of the computer, designing logic for factory automation, and one part on the floor of various plants and factories commissioning and testing. Honestly, while I do enjoy the work sometimes, I could do without the travel that is required, as I'm more of a homebody than anything, plus when I travel, it's never really to anywhere interesting. I'm on the lookout for other jobs that would sit me at home more often!
